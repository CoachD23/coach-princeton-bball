About the Princeton Offense System

By Coach Lee DeForest • January 20, 2024

5 min read • Video included

Why Princeton Offense?

The Princeton Offense was developed by coaching genius Pete Carril at Princeton University. It is a read-based offense that relies on player movement, cutting, and ball reversal to create high-percentage scoring opportunities. It doesn't require elite athleticism — it requires basketball IQ, unselfishness, and a willingness to make the extra pass.

The Princeton Offense is one of the most effective offensive systems in basketball. It has been used at every level of the game — from high school to the NBA. Coaches love it because it creates open shots through ball movement and player reads, not isolation plays or set plays that defenders can scout.

Pete Carril's Princeton Tigers famously upset UCLA in the 1996 NCAA Tournament using this system. Since then, the Princeton Offense has been adopted by coaches worldwide who want to maximize their team's potential regardless of athletic ability.

Who Is This For?

Whether you're a high school coach looking for an offense that maximizes your roster, a college assistant breaking down film, or a youth coach who wants to teach the right way to play basketball — the Princeton Offense is for you. This system teaches players how to read the defense, make decisions, and play unselfish basketball.

You don't need 5-star recruits. You need players who are willing to work hard, move without the ball, and trust the system. That's the beauty of the Princeton Offense — it works at every level because it's built on fundamentals, not athleticism.
